Like most people that I've met, I never found the idea of coding to be enjoyable. It always looked confusing and impossible to understand. Now don't get me wrong, coding still looks confusing and sometimes impossible to understand, but I think the problem most people have is actually putting in the time and effort to learn. This goes for any skill or hobby. The trick is not being smart, it's being consistent.

When thinking about what career paths to follow, I knew it had to be in the tech space, as I enjoy using tech and learning about tech. One day, I got reccomended a "Day In the Life of a Software Engineer". I was bored, so I clicked on it. This man was living my dream life. Nice place, remote job working for amazon, living alone. It was after I finished watching this video that I started to think "Learning to code would be hell, but to be honest learning anything would be hell." So the next day, I searched up "How to learn coding", and here I am making a whole website!

I struggle with consistency a lot. That's why I decided to go slow and easy. I decided that if I watched atleast 10 minutes of the youtube course everyday, I didn't have to do anything else. This method is called Spaced Repetition. It allowed me to willingly click on the youtube course everyday, as the idea of watching a 10 Minute segement didn't sound to bad. Space Repetition also allowed me to finish the course while still remembering and understanding what I actually learned.
